Convictions

I am a Christian / Disciple. But I do not follow blindly what scriptures say. I scrutinize them and pay close attention to detail.

I decided to become a Christian after careful consideration of evidences for Christianity and decided that since there is no evidence that refutes Christianity or the existence of God, I only have to believe one or the other. I chose to believe what the biblical scriptures say because I believe that they are an accurate picture of how I need to live my life and how I must relate to God at a very personal level.

I believe that, in order to become a Christian, one must first be a disciple of Christ, repenting of sins and dedicating to live God's way (Acts: all conversions). Then, after becoming a disciple, this person must then be baptized in water and spirit (John 3:1-7, Acts 2:38, Romans 6:1-4), in the name of the Father, of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Finally, this new Christian must then go and make disciples of all other nations. (Matthew 28:18-20)
