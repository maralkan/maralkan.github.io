Min / Max Thermometer Temperature

  • records the lowest temperature in a given period
  • The minimum thermometer contains alcohol
    • The minimum thermometer has an index, a wire
    • The alcohol, when there is a decrease in temperature, moves the index down
    • But the alcohol is not strong enough to move the index back up
  • The max thermometer contains mercury
    • There is an indicator that the mercury can push up
    • This indicator doesn't pull that indicator back down

Temperature measurement in different atmospheric layers

There are major differences in temperature readings from thermometer.
Near the surface of the earth, we have air that collides with a thermometer. Lots of air molecules collide.
However, in high layers of the atmosphere, there are simply too few air molecules to collide with a thermometer. High readings on thermometers correspond to heating by radiation from the sun. Molecules in this region are actually moving quite fast, but there are too few of them, so it feels cold.

  • Meteorologists look at lines of equal air pressure by taking in reports from many weather stations. Wind

  • What causes the initial movement of air?
    • The pressure gradient. It's the difference between areas of high air pressure and low air pressure. If you're running into the wind, you're moving into a place of higher air pressure and if the wind is pushing you, you're moving into lower air pressure areas.
    • The closer the isobars get, the faster the wind will blow.
    • If the pressure gradient is very steep then the wind will move very fast
    • Example: Hurricane
      • The eye of the storm is an area of low pressure. So the air rises in the eye and it falls in the storm, where the air pressure is very high.
  • What influences wind direction?
    • Coriolis Effect
    • Some of the air that touches the surface of the earth is going to move as a result of friction.
    • In the Northern hemisphere, wind will deflect to the right and the in the Southern hemisphere, the wind will deflect to the left.
    • Wind Deflection Patterns
      • In the Northern hemisphere, a high pressure air parcel will have air flowing out to the right (֍, ↻). A low pressure air parcel will have air moving into it from the right. ֍, ↺
      • Southern hemisphere, ֎ H: ↺, L: ↻
  • Wind Speed-Direction
  • Air Mass Convergence / Divergence
  • When you have converging wind closer to the surface of the earth, and diverging wind higher up, then you have rising air.
  • If you have converging wind at high altitudes and diverging wind near the surface of the air, you have sinking air.
  • Prevailing wind
  • It can help determine where to place things in a city. A prevailing wind can be a significant factor in determing placement of landfills, city centers, and even house planning / building. The direction of the wind will influence in which direction pollutants may fly, where city centers would have the least amount of wind, where a house should be better insulated so that it can receive better insulation during the winter and receive ventilation during the summer.
