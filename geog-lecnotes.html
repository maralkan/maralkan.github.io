Daylight-Savings Time

  • Move clocks ahead by an hour during the summer months ... "Spring Ahead; Fall Back!"
  • Originally used by Germans during WWII; now practiced by many nations
  • Conserves lighting energy by providing an extra hour of daylight
  • Arizona and Hawaii are the only states that don't follow this system
    • The Navajo Nation, however, about 20% of Arizona, do use the DS system
    • The Hopi reservation, a reservation surrounded by the Navajo nation does not follow the system just because the Navajo do

Earth-Sun Relations

Earth's Revolution around the Sun

  • One revolution takes 365 days, 5 hours, 49 minutes
  • Elliptical Orbit
    • Aphelion 93.6 million miles (152,171,500 km)
    • Perihelion 93 million miles (147,166,480 km)
    • Average distance 93 million miles (149,597,892 km)
  • Earth at perihelion during Northern Hemisphere winter; aphelion during Northern Hemisphere summer
  • 23.5 degrees off of the elliptical orbit plane

Annual March of the Seasons

  • Three important conditions:
    • Declination of the Sun
    • Solar altitude
    • Length of day
  • Two solstices
    • June solstice
    • December solstice
      • Occurs on approximately December 22 each year
      • Sun is directly overhead at 23.5 degree S latitude
      • Arctic Circle in 24 hours of darkness
      • Marks start of winter in Northen Hemisphere; summer in Southern Hemisphere
  • Two equinoxes
    • March equinox
    • September equinox
  • Significance of seasonal patterns
    • Spread of solar rays over small and large areas
    • Tropical latitudes consistently wamer
    • Polar regions consistently cooler

Where is the sun is overhead at different times of the year

  • On 3/21, 9/22 sun is directly over the equator
  • On 6/21, sun is directly over Tropic of Cancer
  • On 12/22, sun is directly over Tropic of Capricorn
