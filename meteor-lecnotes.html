Question: What is it that pushes the air down the leeway side of a mountain

  • Good example: Santa Ana winds
    • wind keeps moving in the same direction
    • difference between air pressure keeps the wind moving down
    • we have a basin that tends to exacerbate the compression
    • the wind will eventually dissipate due to the wet side having less pressure
  • counter-example: the Mistral in France
    • sometimes the air is so cold that despite heating by compression, the air remains cold

Question: what is causing the warmer temperatures near the 30 degrees North/South?

  • Atmospheric Circulation
    • At these latitudes, the air circulation is pushing air down at these latitudes, thereby heating them up.

Min / Max Thermometer Temperature

  • records the lowest temperature in a given period
  • The minimum thermometer contains alcohol
    • The minimum thermometer has an index, a wire
    • The alcohol, when there is a decrease in temperature, moves the index down
    • But the alcohol is not strong enough to move the index back up
  • The max thermometer contains mercury
    • There is an indicator that the mercury can push up
    • This indicator doesn't pull that indicator back down

Temperature measurement in different atmospheric layers

There are major differences in temperature readings from thermometer

Near the surface of the earth, we have air that collides with a thermometer. Lots of air molecules collide

However, in high layers of the atmosphere, there are simply too few air molecules to collide with a thermometer. High readings on thermometers correspond to heating by radiation from the sun. Molecules in this region are actually moving quite fast, but there are too few of them, so it feels cold.
